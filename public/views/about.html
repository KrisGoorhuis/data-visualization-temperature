About

I used to write words about video games. Now I write code because of them! The self-teaching process began around the time freelance pitching wore thin and folks described some of my favorite puzzle games as evoking the same gratification as coding. The moment I finished my first basic word-reversing script I knew they were right.

I've since run through the excellent freeCodeCamp.com front end curriculum and picked up back end tech on my own to grab a handle on the full MEAN stack, knocked out some fun practice and portfolio projects, and reigned myself in from diving headlong into Golang. I should probably finish this career switch before spending too much time exploring other cool things, hey?

Regardless of where I find myself in the immediate future, tapping away at Christmas-light text editors and engineering logic and mechanisms is just gosh darned fun. It satisfies both the mind and the giddy twelve year old, been challenging and rewarding in equal measure. I'm excited to forge ahead.
